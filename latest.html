Photography Write-Off Guide

100% sourced to IRS publications and IRC sections. Every deduction includes what records you must keep.

What You Can Deduct

Camera Bodies, Lenses & Gear ⓘ

100%

Camera bodies, RF lenses, flashes, tripods, bags, filters, batteries, memory cards.

Records needed

Receipt + date placed in service (when you first used it for work)

Software Subscriptions ⓘ

100%

Adobe Creative Cloud, Lightroom presets, Pixieset, Pic-Time, HoneyBook, Dubsado, Canva Pro, This app.

Records needed

Subscription invoices / bank statements

Business Mileage ⓘ

67¢/mi

Driving to photo shoots, client meetings, engagement sessions, equipment runs, second shooter meetups.

Records needed

Mileage log: date, starting point, destination, purpose, miles. MileIQ or Stride app recommended.

Home Office ⓘ

$5/sq ft

Dedicated editing room or workspace used exclusively and regularly for business. Max 300 sq ft = $1,500/yr.

Records needed

Floor plan or diagram showing room dimensions + photo of the exclusive-use workspace

Business Meals (with clients) ⓘ

50%

Consultation meals with couples, engagement shoot planning lunches, styled shoot coordination meals.

Records needed

Receipt + note: who was present, what business was discussed

Education & Workshops ⓘ

100%

Photography workshops, presets/posing courses, business coaching, editing tutorials, online education.

Receipt + syllabus or course description showing it relates to your photography business

What You Cannot Deduct

Personal Clothing & Appearance

0%

Clothing you wear to shoots is NOT deductible unless it is a distinctive uniform that cannot be worn as everyday clothing. Haircuts, makeup for yourself, and dry cleaning personal clothes are strictly personal expenses.

Authority: Reg. §1.262-1(b)(8) | Pevsner v. Commissioner (5th Cir. 1980)

Entertainment (Post-2017)

0%

The Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (2017) eliminated the entertainment deduction. Taking a client to a concert, sports game, or similar event is no longer deductible — even if business is discussed.

Authority: IRC §274(a)(1) as amended by TCJA 2017

Family Meals

0%

Meals with family members are not deductible unless they are bona fide employees of your LLC and business is being conducted.

Authority: IRC §274(a) | IRS Pub 463

Commuting Miles

0%

Driving from home to a regular fixed place of business is commuting — not deductible. However, since most photographers work from home, driving from home to ANY shoot location is a business trip, not a commute.

Authority: IRC §162 | Rev. Rul. 99-7

The Gray Zone — Commonly Misunderstood

Personal Phone (Mixed Use) ⓘ

Business % only

Track your usage split. A dedicated LLC business line gives you 100% with zero documentation burden.

Mixed Personal/Business Travel ⓘ

Business days only

Flight is 100% deductible if the primary purpose is business. Hotel for personal days is not deductible.

Home Internet (Mixed Use) ⓘ

Business % only

Estimate your business-use percentage. Many photographers can reasonably claim 60-80%.

Audit-Proofing Best Practices

  • The $75 Receipt Rule

    IRS requires written records for any expense over $75. Digital photos of receipts are valid. Photograph them immediately using your notes app or Expensify.

  • Separate Bank Account (Required)

    Commingling personal and business funds is the #1 way to lose LLC liability protection and invite IRS scrutiny. All business income and expenses must flow through your LLC account.

  • Mileage Log (Year-Round)

    The IRS requires a contemporaneous mileage log — meaning recorded at or near the time of each trip. Reconstructing from memory at year-end will not survive an audit. Use MileIQ or Stride.

  • 7-Year Record Retention

    Keep all receipts, invoices, bank statements, and mileage logs for at least 3 years (6 years if you underreported income by 25%+). The IRS recommends 7 years to be safe.
